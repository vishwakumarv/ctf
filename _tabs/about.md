Hi, I'm Vishwa.

I'm an Electronics and Communication Engineering student focused on cybersecurity, reverse engineering, Linux systems, and low-level understanding of how real-world systems actually work — and sometimes break. My interests currently revolve around offensive security, embedded systems, Android analysis, network behavior, and building tools that make technical workflows less painful.

Most of my work starts with curiosity:
- breaking down applications,
- understanding protocols,
- debugging random system behavior at 2AM,
- or building projects that probably became larger than originally intended.

I spend most of my time around Linux environments, Python, Bash, virtualization, reverse engineering tools, and security research workflows. I also enjoy experimenting with embedded systems and Raspberry Pi–based projects where hardware and software start overlapping in weird ways.

Some things I've worked on include:
- **ReDroid-AI** — a reverse engineering assistant focused on Android application analysis and automated reporting workflows
- **DEADPIXEL** — a phishing simulation research toolkit built for controlled security-awareness demonstrations
- **PuBOT** — a Raspberry Pi–based embedded robotics project involving autonomous navigation and sensor integration

Outside academics, I enjoy CTFs, writeups, debugging, open-source tools, and exploring how systems behave internally instead of just using them from the surface level.

This website is basically my corner of the internet for:
- technical writeups,
- project logs,
- cybersecurity experiments,
- reverse engineering notes,
- and occasional chaos.
